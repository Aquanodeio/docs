---
title: How It Works
description: An overview of the core services powering the Aquanode platform.
---

Aquanode is powered by three internal services — **Mjolnir**, **Themis**, and **Ogre** — each with a distinct role, working in concert to keep your deployments fast, accurate, and reliable.

---

## Mjolnir — Unified GPU API

When you deploy a VM on Aquanode, you don't talk to a provider directly — you talk to Mjolnir. It is the central API layer that abstracts every GPU provider (web2 datacenters, web3 networks, and community providers) behind a single unified interface.

- Routes your deployment to the right provider based on availability, pricing, and GPU type
- Abstracts away provider-specific APIs and access rules
- Makes adding new providers seamless without any change to the platform surface

Everything starts here. Mjolnir is the entry point for all compute.

---

## Themis — Reconciliation Service

Once Mjolnir provisions resources, Themis steps in to make sure what's happening on the provider side matches Aquanode's internal state — continuously.

- Reconciles your account balance and resource usage against live provider data from Mjolnir
- Catches and corrects any drift before it affects billing or deployment state
- Ensures what you see on the Aquanode console always reflects reality

Themis is what makes the system trustworthy at scale — without it, discrepancies would compound silently.

---

## Ogre — Worker Service

While Mjolnir handles provisioning and Themis handles consistency, Ogre handles everything that keeps your deployment healthy day-to-day.

- Continuously monitors **GPU health** and **resource access** across active VMs
- Collects and surfaces **real-time metrics** for your deployments
- Runs the underlying machinery for **backups** (automated and manual snapshots) and **migrations** (moving workloads across GPUs or providers)

Ogre is the operational backbone — it acts on the state that Mjolnir and Themis maintain.

---

Together, these three services form a closed loop: Mjolnir provisions, Themis reconciles, and Ogre operates — giving you a GPU cloud that is reliable, consistent, and observable without manual intervention.
